Our beloved campus
The winter storm had transported 
Into a new, ephemeral dimension

The roofs of buildings, plastered with snow
As if covered with a thick coat of fresh paint

The roads and walkways slumbered
Under an endless blanket of pure white
Awaiting the day they might awaken to light 
And be tread upon once again

Was this really the same place we had known?
I think not
Just as paint is spread upon a canvas
Or words written across a page
Something entirely new is born

But just as spring follows winter
This transitory existence must come to an end
For a wondrous, frozen dimension
Is not the domain of students
