Today, while brushing my hair
Whimsical music was made
Powered by static electricity
As the brush glided across my head

If I moved my hand quickly—
Zip! Crackle!
A wonderful, high-pitched sound
Like a warm coat’s zipper
Or faint pouring rain

If I moved slowly—
Crackle, crackle, pop
An equally bizarre and pleasant lower tone
It could act as the bass in a symphony of static

It was then that I realized
I had become a musical instrument
Or perhaps I’ve already been one this whole time
For a song is always within my soul

In any case
The music performed by the orchestra 
Of my hair, the brush, and the static
Along with the resulting fluffiness of my hair
Was truly a delightful experience
