The two sit upon a small grassy hill overlooking the plains. Behind them stands the old apple tree, its waving leaves and branches casting a pleasant shade over them. Nutrients fill the soil, which, in turn, provide the tree with strength and vitality. The tree then bears fruit, and the two partake whenever they please. They need not worry, for here they are always provided for.

It is warm here. It is safe.

Irene sits with her legs tucked up against herself, clutching them with her interlocked hands. Maria is more relaxed, her arm draped over her sister’s shoulders in an affectionate embrace.

“Hey. I think today’s the day. Are you excited?” asks Maria.

“...” Irene gazes down at the ground in front of her.

“What’s wrong? Are you nervous?” Maria tilts her head to the side to look her sister in the eyes. And such wondrous eyes they are, though they presently hold an air of melancholy.

“Yeah, it’s just-” Irene starts, “We have no idea what’s out there, right? What if it’s cold? Or painful? What if we don’t have anything to eat?”

Maria smiles at her sister and giggles softly. “Oh, sister of mine. I feel like you’re forgetting something.”

“Oh yeah? And what’s that?”

Maria raises her hand in front of them and begins a slow, outward-sweeping motion. As if she were painting the horizon with her palm.

“Just look at these green fields. And those blue skies. Look at this apple tree above us.”

They both turn their gazes upward. The fruitful branches wave from side to side in the refreshing breeze. The apples look ripe and delicious. The rustling leaves murmur a beautiful song. The sisters look back at each other and giggle.

“You see? If someone out there didn’t care for us, do you think we would have all this great stuff?”

“Haha! Yeah. I guess you’re right,” says Irene.

Maria shuffles over and takes hold of her sister’s hand. “And you know, sis. No matter what happens out there…whatever cold, or hunger, or pain…I’ll always be there for you.”

Irene smiles as tears begin welling up in her eyes. “Yeah. I know. I feel the same way!”

“Heehee! Of course!” Maria starts patting her sister on the back. “So I think it’s gonna be fun! We should go meet whoever’s waiting for us!”

The sisters help each other up off the ground and stand, hand-in-hand, looking toward the sunrise.
Together, they begin walking toward the light.
