––– Chapter 1 –––

One night, thousands of years ago, a falling star crashed into the Earth. It was known as The Star Too Great For The Sky. An adventurous young girl named Sera went to explore the Starfall Crater. She lived in a town not too far from the impact site. However, the village Elder had forbidden anyone from approaching it.

“That star is cursed!” he would proclaim. “It is a terrible omen of death! We must immediately send for the king’s orders on this matter!”

But Sera did not see it that way. That night, she gazed in wonder at the heavens, which were set aflame in brilliant hues of golden light. The Great Golden Star streaked across the sky, flocked by countless lesser meteors of sapphire hue that soared alongside it. Yet, the Great Star was the only one to survive its fiery descent, as the smaller blue comets either soared away into the outer void or burned up into nothingness. Thus, the night sky could hold back The Star no longer, and it crashed into the ground with a giant golden explosion. It was the most enchanting thing Sera had ever seen.

So, the next night, she snuck out of town and embarked on a journey. Since the Crater’s location was a few leagues away from Enir Village, Sera prepared various supplies and equipment for the trip. The girl brought with her a leather knapsack, a lantern, a tin of shortbread biscuits, and a drinking canteen. She also had gifts from her father: a dark blue cloak and a kris dagger with an undulating blade. Set into the brooch of the cloak and embedded into the cross-guard of the kris were gemstones of golden amber. These gifts were Sera’s most treasured items.

The journey would be harrowing even for an adult, but the young girl never lost heart. In fact, she could feel something stirring deep within her soul, beckoning her toward The Star. So Sera trod on through dim valleys and forests of shadow. She ascended rocky hills, drank from flowing streams, and rested in shallow caves.

As she traveled, the little girl used the light of the lantern and the faint glow of her blade to ward off the night creatures. Her father had always told her that the weapon held magical power and would guard her against all manner of evil. She never understood exactly what he meant until now. In the dark of night, Sera could not fully make out the forms of the beasts. But she could see their glowing eyes and hear their growling and hissing whenever she pointed her kris toward them.

Eventually, more and more eyes and snarling surrounded her and started creeping closer. A multitude of malevolent shadows was growing. One little girl could not possibly ward off all those night creatures alone. The Elder had told Sera countless tales about such monsters. Tales of children who get lost while playing in the woods and are never seen again. She believed them to be merely fairy tales to scare kids out of wandering too far from the village. She never thought it would come to this.

A guttural bark from right behind made Sera whip around. When she held up her lantern, she could see it. Haphazardly illuminated in the light of the swinging lantern was a dark, snarling face of fierce eyes and jagged, bared teeth. A Shaduwolf.

Sera turned and ran.

In her frenzy, the lantern was dropped. She sprinted aimlessly through the pitch-black forest, wildly swinging her weapon, as it was the only source of light and protection.

She ran and ran until her legs gave out underneath her. She collapsed onto the ground with a great crash and lay there, unable to muster the energy to pick herself up.

The wolves must not have been too far behind.

“No! I can't do this!” she cried angrily while striking the earth beneath her. “I just wanted to adventure like we used to! I’m sorry, I—I just miss you so much, Father.”

The young girl choked back a sob.

“I wish I could’ve known what Mother was like, too…She must have been wonderful.”

Suddenly, Sera noticed the kris, which had dropped in front of her during the tumble. The blade and its gemstone began shining even brighter than before, so much so that they lit up the forest around her. The tall stalks of the illuminated evergreens stretched toward the heavens.

The amber gem embedded in the cross-guard now gave the impression of a brilliant shooting star, with the wavy blade appearing as its beaming tail. It was then that Sera understood the name bestowed upon the dagger. Father had called it Veiled Starlight.

She held up the kris in front of her and turned it in her hand. The sight of it was utterly captivating. All her fear and fatigue were washed away in the radiance of the magical blade. Then, Sera suddenly noticed light coming from something ahead, too. As she walked toward the source, she realized that her journey was complete. Before her eyes was a great depression in the ground that looked to be as wide as her village and deep enough to fit a watchtower. She had reached the Starfall Crater.
